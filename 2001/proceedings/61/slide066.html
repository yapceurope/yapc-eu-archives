Handcuffing

Problem: You're asked to write a program to do something you know nothing about.

Solution: Handcuff a customer to the programming project

  • The customer is given explicit time to work with you

  • If the project fails, its now both your faults

  • Move the customer right next to the programmers.

As a result...

  • Physical proximity means better communication, fast answers.

  • Less blame transferal.

  • Customer intimately aware of the details of the project whether they want to be or not.

Works best on small, in-house projects.
